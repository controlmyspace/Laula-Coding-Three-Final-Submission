POEM I / AN ODE TO TAIMAS

"I'm taking the dog for a walk".
But more often than not,
I think our dogs are taking us humans out for a walk.

I pull him the direction and pace I want to go.

But I should let him lead,
Let him control the pace,

So I too can learn to smell the plants
and chase the bugs.

POEM II /ON IMMIGRATION (2022)

It’s easier to learn to love the environment
  the nature first

But it’s harder to learn to love the people

Assimilate?
   No

Appreciate?
   Yes

Respect?
  Hopefully

I have since learned
  to love the space surrounding me
   that I used to detest

I have not yet learned
  to love the people surrounding me
   that I still hold hate for

I have to forgive. Within myself.
Let go. For myself.

POEM III / HAPPY BDAY!

Happy bday dear friend
Since the ripe age of four
I remember choosing you
A gut feeling from my very core
As a friend necessary to pursue

Although we have disconnected
we have always reconnected
despite the many years passing by
via letters, email or through the sky(pe)

miles of separation, even in one city
our lives have separated more & more
But I’m glad to truely always know
That we hold onto dearly what we had since four

building worlds and characters
that we carried in a plastic file
Perhaps my first creative collaboration
That informed the rest of our lives

I wish you curiosity
Creativity and strength
So the happiest of bdays my dear friend,
I'm wishing you a life of love, light and gratitude

POEM IV / POST-SOVIET RELIGION

My family once knew you
I know it in my name

But then where were you
When the reds came

Years passed
And the reds were joined
By a white and blue

And you saw your cue
No longer reduced to the past
Or whispers in hushed tones

We were hesitant to welcome you
We still are
Necessary time to get reacquainted

But your good nature is being misappropriated
Misused
Misplaced

And most dangerously
Misunderstood

Confusion is rife
